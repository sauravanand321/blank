History of the Cricket World Cup Winners

The ICC Men's Cricket World Cup is the pinnacle of One Day International (ODI) cricket, bringing together the best teams from around the globe. Held every four years, it's a celebration of talent, strategy, and unforgettable moments. Since its inception in 1975, the tournament has crowned several champions, each with their own unique story of victory.

List of Winners

  • 1975: West Indies
  • 1979: West Indies
  • 1983: India
  • 1987: Australia
  • 1992: Pakistan
  • 1996: Sri Lanka
  • 1999: Australia
  • 2003: Australia
  • 2007: Australia
  • 2011: India
  • 2015: Australia
  • 2019: England
  • 2023: Australia

Australia holds the record for the most titles, showcasing their dominance in the tournament's history. India's memorable wins in 1983 and 2011, and West Indies' early dominance, remain iconic moments for cricket fans.
